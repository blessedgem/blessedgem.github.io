Research

My research investigates how cybersecurity compliance can move from static, manual, and document-heavy processes toward automated, auditable, privacy-preserving, and governance-enforced systems.

Dissertation Direction

Cybersecurity Policy Compliance Automation for Critical Information Infrastructure

This research explores a framework that combines permissioned blockchain, smart contracts, generative AI, large language models, and privacy-preserving verification to support cybersecurity policy compliance automation for critical information infrastructure.

Core Research Themes

1. Ledger-Governed Compliance Workflows

Designing smart contract workflows that enforce assessment creation, evidence submission, assessor review, endorsement, finalization, and audit traceability across multiple organizations.

2. Privacy-Preserving Verification

Exploring how vendors can prove selected compliance claims without disclosing sensitive raw evidence, using approaches such as zero-knowledge proofs and on-chain proof receipts.

3. AI-Assisted Compliance Support

Studying how LLMs can support compliance workflows by drafting questions, mapping controls, summarizing evidence, and supporting human reviewers, while remaining non-authoritative and governed by validation controls.

4. Trust and Maturity Measurement

Developing longitudinal, trust-weighted, and maturity-oriented metrics that evaluate participant behavior and compliance performance across assessment cycles.

Representative Research Questions

  • How can smart contracts improve reliability, auditability, and workflow integrity in third-party compliance assessment?
  • How can compliance maturity be measured across repeated assessment cycles?
  • How can privacy-preserving proofs reduce disclosure risk while maintaining audit confidence?
  • How can LLMs support compliance assessment without becoming the final authority?
  • What are the throughput, latency, and governance bottlenecks in blockchain-based compliance workflows?
