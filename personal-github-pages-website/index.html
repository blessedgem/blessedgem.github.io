About Me

I am a Ph.D. researcher at Tennessee Technological University working at the intersection of cybersecurity policy compliance, critical information infrastructure protection, permissioned blockchain, smart contracts, privacy-preserving verification, and AI governance.

My research focuses on building trustworthy compliance automation systems where governance, auditability, privacy, and accountability are enforced through system design rather than only through manual documentation. I am especially interested in third-party risk assessment, Hyperledger Fabric, zero-knowledge proofs, NIST security controls, and human-validated LLM support for compliance workflows.

My broader goal is to develop practical systems that help organizations verify compliance, reduce manual audit burden, protect sensitive data, and support resilient cybersecurity governance across critical sectors.

Research Interests

  • Cybersecurity policy compliance automation
  • Blockchain and smart contracts for auditability
  • Third-party risk assessment and vendor compliance
  • Privacy-preserving verification and zero-knowledge proofs
  • AI governance, AI risk management, and LLM guardrails
  • Critical information infrastructure and smart grid security
