About Me

I am a Ph.D. researcher in Computer Science at Tennessee Technological University, working at the intersection of cybersecurity governance, AI-assisted compliance automation, permissioned blockchain, and privacy-preserving verification. My research develops governed systems that help organizations move from static, document-based compliance toward auditable, automated, and trustworthy cybersecurity assurance.

Before beginning my Ph.D., I worked in national cybersecurity governance and critical information infrastructure protection, contributing to policy implementation, risk assessment, compliance monitoring, incident reporting, and sector-wide cybersecurity resilience initiatives. This practical background motivates my research goal: to build computing systems that make cybersecurity compliance more trustworthy, verifiable, privacy-preserving, and operationally meaningful.

Research Interests

  • Cybersecurity policy compliance automation
  • AI-assisted governance, risk, and compliance systems
  • Permissioned blockchain and smart contracts
  • Privacy-preserving verification and zero-knowledge proofs
  • Third-party risk assessment and vendor compliance
  • Critical information infrastructure protection
  • Trustworthy and explainable AI for security workflows

Current Focus

  • Doctoral Research: Developing a governed third-party compliance assessment protocol on permissioned ledgers with longitudinal maturity modeling and trust-weighted decision-making.
  • Research Systems: Building Hyperledger Fabric-based workflows for vendor registration, evidence anchoring, assessor review, governed finalization, and audit reconstruction.
  • AI Governance: Exploring how LLMs can support regulatory interpretation and compliance question generation while remaining non-authoritative and subject to human validation.
