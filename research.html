Research

My research asks how cybersecurity compliance can become an enforceable, auditable, and privacy-preserving system property rather than a periodic document-driven exercise. I focus on regulated ecosystems where vendors, assessors, regulators, and service providers must coordinate across organizational boundaries while protecting sensitive information.

Dissertation Direction

Smart Contract-Driven and AI-Assisted Compliance Assessment

My dissertation develops a governed third-party compliance assessment protocol for regulated multi-organization ecosystems. The framework uses permissioned blockchain, smart contracts, LLM-assisted regulatory interpretation, and privacy-preserving verification to support trustworthy assessment workflows across vendors, assessors, and regulators.

Research Vision

Risk changes continuously, but compliance processes often remain slow, fragmented, and point-in-time. My work addresses this gap by designing systems that can keep policies current, synchronize participants, preserve evidence integrity, and support governed decisions through tamper-evident transactions and smart contract enforcement.

Core Research Themes

Ledger-Governed Assessment Workflows

I design permissioned blockchain protocols that execute compliance workflows as governed state machines. These workflows support vendor registration, assessment creation, evidence anchoring, assessor voting, and endorsement-gated finalization.

Maturity and Trust as System-Derived State

I investigate how compliance maturity and participant trust can be derived from protocol-verifiable events across repeated assessment cycles, rather than from subjective narratives alone. This supports longitudinal comparison of whether controls are sustained, improved, or degraded over time.

Privacy-Preserving Verification

I explore how vendors can prove selected compliance claims without disclosing sensitive internal evidence. This includes off-chain evidence handling, on-chain cryptographic anchors, proof receipts, and zero-knowledge proof-based verification.

Governed LLM Assistance

I study how large language models can assist with regulatory interpretation and compliance question generation while remaining non-authoritative. In my design, LLM outputs require human validation before they can become ledger-anchored assessment artifacts.
